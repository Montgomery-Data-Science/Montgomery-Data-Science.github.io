Step 1: Understand the Business Question¶

The Business question that this project seeks to answer is whether an individual that has a loan will default and the probability that the individual may default.

Special Business Use Case¶

Know the probability that a bank customer has of defaulting can be helpful to bankers when trying to set interest rates, because they will have a better idea of the risk involved with writing loans to each individual customer.

Step 2: Data Understanding¶

The following are the variables in our dataset that we must deploy in our classification model

Data Dictionary¶


Dependent Variable¶

**Loan Status:** Categorical variable
that expresses if the loan was paid back or defaulted

Independent Variables¶

**Current Loan Amount:** Numeric variable
that represents the amount of the loan that was paid of fully or defaulted
**Term:** Categorical variable
indicating the length of the loan
**Credit Score:** Numeric variable
representing the riskiness of the customer
**Years in current job:** Ordinal variable
that represents the amount of time the customer has spent in their current job
**Home Ownership:** Categorical variable
that indicates whether the customer rents, has a mortgage or completely owns their home
**Annual Income:** Numeric variable
representing the annual income of the customer
**Purpose:** Categorical variable
representing why the customer wants the loan
**Monthly Debt :** Numeric variable
representing monthly debt of the customer
**Years of Credit History:** Numeric variable
representing years since the first entry into the customer’s credit history
**Months since last delinquent:** Numeric variable
representing time since the customers last loan delinquent payment
**Number of Open Accounts:** Numeric variable
representing the number of credit cards the customer currently has
**Number of Credit Problems:** Numeric variable
representing the number of credit problems in the customer's history
**Current Credit Balance:** Numeric variable
representing the current total debt of the customer
**Maximum Open Credit:** Numeric variable
representing the max credit from all credit sources that the customer has
**Bankruptcies:** Numeric variable
representing the number of bankruptcies of customer
**Tax Liens:** Numeric variable
representing the number of Tax Liens of customer


Descriptive Variables¶

**Loan ID:** Used to identify a specific loan; each is unique
**Customer ID:** Used to identify a specific customer; may occur multiple times for multiple loans
